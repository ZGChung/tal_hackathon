# Social Studies Curriculum

## Overview
This social studies curriculum explores human society, history, geography, economics, and civics, helping students understand their place in the world and develop informed citizenship.

## Core Disciplines

### History

#### World History
- Ancient civilizations
- Medieval period
- Renaissance and Reformation
- Age of Exploration
- Modern world history
- Contemporary global issues

#### United States History
- Colonial America
- American Revolution
- Civil War and Reconstruction
- Industrialization
- World Wars
- Civil Rights Movement
- Modern America

### Geography

#### Physical Geography
- Landforms and climate
- Natural resources
- Environmental systems
- Map skills and cartography

#### Human Geography
- Population and migration
- Cultural geography
- Economic geography
- Urban and rural patterns
- Regional studies

### Economics

#### Economic Concepts
- Supply and demand
- Market systems
- Economic institutions
- Personal finance
- Global economics

#### Economic Systems
- Traditional economies
- Market economies
- Command economies
- Mixed economies

### Civics and Government

#### Government Structure
- Forms of government
- Democratic principles
- Constitutional framework
- Separation of powers
- Federalism

#### Citizenship
- Rights and responsibilities
- Civic participation
- Voting and elections
- Public policy
- Community involvement

### Sociology and Anthropology

#### Social Structures
- Family and community
- Social institutions
- Social change
- Cultural diversity

#### Human Behavior
- Social psychology
- Group dynamics
- Social norms
- Cultural practices

### Historical Thinking

- Historical interpretation

### Geographic Thinking

- Place and region
- Human-environment interaction

### Civic Engagement
- Informed citizenship
- Democratic participation
- Community service
- Global awareness

- Economic opportunity
